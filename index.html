Suramadu Bridge: The Iconic Link Between Surabaya and Madura

Completed in 2009, Jembatan Surabaya–Madura—or Suramadu Bridge—transformed travel between Java and Madura, cutting crossing times to minutes and opening new economic horizons across East Java.

Engineering a Nation-Defining Bridge

The Suramadu Bridge began construction on 20 August 2003 and officially opened on 10 June 2009. Spanning the Madura Strait, the structure stretches 5,438 metres and includes cable-stayed sections that soar 146 metres above sea level. Since its toll removal in 2018, the bridge has become a fully accessible lifeline for residents, entrepreneurs, and students commuting between Surabaya and Madura.

Where to Find the Bridge

Suramadu Bridge spans the Madura Strait, linking the city of Surabaya in East Java with Bangkalan Regency on Madura Island. The central navigation span sits 35 metres above sea level, allowing maritime traffic to pass beneath.

Coordinates: -7.1918° S, 112.7471° E
Nearest City: Surabaya, East Java
Opening Year: 2009
Toll Policy: Toll-free since 2018

Benefits for Society and the Economy

Beyond faster journeys, Suramadu Bridge promotes sustainable regional growth. It stimulates investment across logistics, agriculture, and tourism while improving access to education and healthcare for Madura communities.

Economic Diversification

Enhances trade routes for Madura’s salt, cattle, and batik industries, encouraging new value-added businesses.

Inclusive Mobility

Provides 24/7 connectivity that replaces ferry queues with reliable road access for students, workers, and emergency services.

Tourism Growth

Showcases East Java’s maritime landscape, creating opportunities for cultural festivals and coastal tourism initiatives.

Regional Investment

Supports infrastructure projects within the Suramadu Development Zone, attracting public–private partnerships into Madura.
